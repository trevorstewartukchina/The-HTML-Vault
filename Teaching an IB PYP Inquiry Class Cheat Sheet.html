PYP Inquiry: The Teaching Approach

Scaffolding Conceptual Learning through the Inquiry Process

1. Core Philosophy and The Inquiry Cycle

Inquiry is the **leading pedagogical approach** (the HOW we teach). It's **purposeful and authentic**, drawing its fuel from the transdisciplinary themes and the curiosity of the learners.
The process builds **Learner Agency** (Voice, Choice, and Ownership) and is defined by the iterative interplay: **Asking (Inquiry) → Thinking (Reflection) → Doing (Action)**.

The Multi-Layered Process (Kath Murdoch Phases)

1. Tuning In: Activate **prior knowledge** and gather initial thinking/misconceptions (Formative Assessment).
2. Finding Out: Students act as **researchers**, gathering first-hand data using diverse methodologies (developing ATLs).
3. Sorting Out: **Meaning Making**—organize, analyze, interpret, and communicate information. High-level reflection.
4. Going Further: **Personalized pathways**, challenging assumptions, and individual/small group extensions (Differentiation).
5. Concluding & Acting: Stating revised **conceptual understandings** and executing **Action/Transfer**.

2. The Teacher's Role in Guided Inquiry

The teacher is a **facilitator** and **co-investigator**. The PYP emphasizes **Guided Inquiry**, which scaffolds complex conceptual understandings.

A. Explicit Teaching and Facilitation

  • **"Just In Time," Not "Just In Case":** Subject-specific content is taught only when needed (e.g., teaching fractions to analyze a data set for the Central Idea).
  • **Modeling & Scaffolding:** Model inquiry and explicitly teach ATL skills (thinking, research, communication) needed for the inquiry's current phase.
  • **Grouping:** Continually **group and regroup** students based on needs, interests, and relationship-building to support skill mastery and differentiation.

B. Learner Goals (Evidence of Practice)

  • **Build and Adapt Theories:** Students move beyond recalling facts to constructing, communicating, testing, and adapting their own conceptual theories.
  • **Seek New Perspectives:** Students make deliberate links between knowledge and concepts and actively seek out diverse viewpoints.
  • **Transfer & Action:** Students demonstrate understanding by using knowledge in new contexts and initiating **responsible action**.

3. Key Shifts: Moving to Transdisciplinary Inquiry

The shift from conventional to inquiry-based practice is non-negotiable for success in evaluation:

Focus Area Move FROM (Conventional/Multidisciplinary) Move TOWARDS (Inquiry/Transdisciplinary)
**Time/Schedule** Fixed time frames; schedule revolves around subject lessons. **Open-ended time**; schedule **flexibly accommodates** the inquiry flow.
**Curriculum** Linear process (Start → Finish). Concepts/Questions find pre-determined answers. **Iterative process** (Asking → Thinking → Doing). Concepts are used to **construct new understandings**.
**Skills (ATL)** Skills taught in isolation ("Just in case"). Skills developed in **authentic contexts** ("Just in time").
**Action/Agency** Learner action planned by the teachers at the start. Learners as recipients. **Learner-initiated action, emerging throughout** the inquiry. Learners are active partners.

4. Transfer, Action, and Community

Action: The Core of Transfer

Action is the **transfer of learning** to make a positive social and/or ecological impact. This responsible action is the ultimate proof of **conceptual understanding**—students don't just know something; they use it to make a difference.

The Role of the Learning Community

Community engagement ensures the inquiry remains authentic, relevant, and connected to the wider world.

  • **Community Experts:** Serve as content and methodological resources in the Finding Out and Going Further phases.
  • **Family Members:** Support the process by discussing the **value of the inquiry**, modeling communication, and helping with research.
  • **Local/Global Groups:** Provide authentic contexts for students to **take action** and apply their learning in the real world.

Inquiry is a rigorous process of continuous learning, transforming the role of both teacher and learner.
